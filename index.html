💡 Pricing Principles
From Module 6: How to Talk About Money
Price from value, not time
What is the transformation worth to your client? A $10k result justifies a $2k price. Stop trading hours for dollars.
Stop apologising for your price
State your price clearly. Pause. Let them respond. The discomfort you feel is normal — it gets easier every time you hold your rate.
Explain your value, not your hours
"This includes X, Y, and Z, which together help you achieve [outcome]." Not: "I spend about 4 hours per week on this."
Handle hesitation with confidence
"I understand it's an investment. What I can tell you is that clients who commit fully always see results. What's holding you back?"

🌸 Modules 4, 8 & 10 — Show Up · Email · Get Seen
Audience Builder Guide
A step-by-step guide to growing your audience across social media, email, and beyond. Work through each phase across your 12 months.
Module 4 — Where Should You Show Up?
You don't need to be everywhere. You need to be somewhere consistently.
Choose ONE primary platform
Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, or TikTok. Ask: Where do my ideal clients already spend time? Go there. Master it before adding another.
The 3-type content formula
Educate: Teach something valuable (40%)
Connect: Share your story and personality (40%)
Offer: Tell people how to work with you (20%)
Consistency beats volume
3 posts per week for 12 months beats 30 posts in January and burning out. Choose a frequency you can sustain, then stick to it.
No chasing trends
Trends fade. Your message doesn't. Build a body of content around your expertise and your ideal client's real problems.
My Social Media Plan
Module 8 — Start Building an Email List
You need one. No complicated funnels. Just foundation.
Why you need an email list
Social media accounts can disappear overnight. Your email list is an asset you own. Even 200 engaged subscribers outperforms 5,000 passive followers.
Create something simple to offer
A one-page checklist, a short guide, a resource list — something genuinely useful that your ideal client would want enough to give you their email for.
What to send
One email per week is enough. Share something valuable, a story, or a behind-the-scenes. Always end with one clear call to action.
Recommended platforms
Mailchimp (free to start), ConvertKit / Kit (great for creators), Flodesk (beautiful templates). Pick one and start. Perfection is not the goal.

Module 10 — Get Seen Beyond Social Media
Simple PR basics. How to build credibility. Visibility with purpose.
Podcasts
Pitch yourself as a guest on podcasts your ideal clients listen to. You don't need a big following — you need a clear topic and a good pitch.
Collaborations
Partner with complementary businesses for joint webinars, Instagram lives, or email swaps. Their audience becomes your audience.
Speaking & Events
Local networking events, online summits, virtual workshops. Every time you speak you build credibility and attract new leads.
Referrals
Ask happy clients to refer you. Make it easy — give them exact language to use. A warm referral converts at 5x the rate of cold outreach.

🤝 Module 9 — When Do You Need Help?
Hire a VA
Knowing when and how to hire a Virtual Assistant is one of the biggest growth moves you'll make. This tool helps you figure out what you need, write the job ad, and hire with confidence.
Module 9 — When Do You Need Help?
When to hire
When a task takes you more than 2 hours per week and doesn't require your specific expertise — that task belongs to a VA. Your time is worth more than inbox management.
When not to hire
Don't hire before you have consistent revenue to cover the cost. Don't hire to avoid tasks you haven't systemised yet — a VA needs a process to follow.
What to track financially
Know your numbers before hiring. Can you cover VA costs from one client payment? Budget for 5-10 hours/week at $25-50/hour to start.
Speak to a lawyer or accountant first
Before you hire: understand contractor vs employee classification in your country. Get advice on contracts, tax implications, and how to pay properly.
